collisonal sum 

The sum \( S = \sum_{k=0}^{N} r^k \) where \( r = 1 - \frac{2 m_e}{M} \):

1. \( S = 1 + r + r^2 + \cdots + r^N \)
2. Multiply by \( r \): \( rS = r + r^2 + \cdots + r^{N+1} \)
3. Subtract: \( S - rS = 1 - r^{N+1} \)
4. \( S (1 - r) = 1 - r^{N+1} \)
5. \( S = \frac{1 - r^{N+1}}{1 - r} \)

Substitute \( r = 1 - \delta \), \( \delta = \frac{2 m_e}{M} \):
\( 1 - r = \delta \), so:

\[
S = \frac{1 - (1 - \delta)^{N+1}}{\delta}
\]

Thus:

\[
\epsilon = e E \lambda \cdot \frac{1 - \left(1 - \frac{2 m_e}{M}\right)^{N+1}}{\frac{2 m_e}{M}}
\]
