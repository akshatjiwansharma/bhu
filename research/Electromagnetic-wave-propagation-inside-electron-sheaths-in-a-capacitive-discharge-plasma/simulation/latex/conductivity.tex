The \textbf{imaginary part} of the conductivity is given by:

\[
	\sigma_{\text{imag}} = \frac{n_e e^2 \omega}{m_e \left( \nu^2 + \omega^2 \right)}
\]


%The \textbf{real part} of the conductivity is:
\[
\sigma_{\text{real}} = \frac{n_e e^2 \nu}{m_e \left( \nu^2 + \omega^2 \right)}

\]

\mathbf{J} = \sigma \mathbf{E}

P = \sigma E^2


%where:
\begin{align*}
n_e & \text{ is the electron density (m$^{-3}$)}, \\
e & \text{ is the electron charge (C)}, \\
m & \text{ is the electron mass (kg)}, \\
\omega & \text{ is the angular frequency (rad/s)}, \\
\nu & \text{ is the collision frequency (Hz)}.
\end{align*}
